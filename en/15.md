# Afterword

Every piece I create, whether it's a course or a book, is essentially an extension of the "Li Xiaolai's Writing Class" that I teach in the "Abundant Life" community. It's like a real-world case study for my students.

The folks in my community have seen it all over the years — Li Xiaolai's relentless creativity, the "tear it down and start over" approach, and the constant revisions after the first draft is done. We're all on the same page that the idea of "writing like a genius" is just a myth. As I keep hammering home in this book, we know that great work is 99% made up of the "boring and monotonous yet essential" stuff.

All my nonfiction works, whether courses or books, follow a simple structure — always have, always will:

> * **Concept** — Dive deep
> * **Problem** — Solution

I don't fuss over "techniques" or "style." For instance, I stick to analogies and parallelism, skipping other rhetorical devices. I'm not even that concerned about "word choice," like distinguishing between "conversational" and "formal" language. The priority is to get the point across clearly.

I don't sweat defining my "audience" either. To me, it's always been clear. First and foremost, I write for myself — all my content is essentially my own study notes. Then, it's for those around me, like my family and my community members. That's it. Whether others stumble upon it is their business.

This time, though, I borrowed a so-called "advanced" storytelling technique: "multi-threaded narrative."

The main thread is "how to effectively use generative AI like ChatGPT based on large language models." Then there are several subplots, like "general tool methodology," "thinking paths," "programmatic thinking," "production and creation," and "equalizing tools."

But in the end, whether it's the main thread or the subplots, they all serve one core purpose: "to transform you into a different kind of person":

> **Someone who can afford, activate, and apply artificial intelligence.**

If the writing class students can "learn again" from this new book — and there's plenty to learn — there's one section worth highlighting: Chapter 3, Section 7:

> 3.7. Depth and Tenacity

If you can combine this with the "super professional" section from the writing class, you'll naturally gain a deeper understanding of where "quality content" comes from.

Of course, the objective standard remains unchanged:

> **Is what I wrote genuinely useful?**

After finishing, I keep asking myself. The answer is a resounding yes — it's genuinely useful for me and those around me. Not just now, but five, ten years down the line. The utility of this work will only grow as AI evolves.

— I guess that's good enough.

Here's to our continued good fortune!

 — Xiaolai Li

Beijing, October 2024
